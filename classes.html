CLASSES

Classes are held at the International District Chinatown Community Center in Seattle, Washington.
We teach a variety of classic kung-fu fighting techniques through the neoclassical style of Baat Ying Batt Faat (Eight Animals and Eight Methods).
By blending traditional kicking, striking, and grappling methods with modern fighting theories, our Si-Jo (founder) Allen Chinn
has created a system of Chinese Martial arts that respects tradition while staying relevant for today's practitioner.

CLASSES

We use the sun-moon salute and standard stretches to open class. Our warm-up routine is a sequence of kicking, punching, and stance training.
The training portion varies according to the season, level of experience, and interests of the students. Training can include traditional forms,
weapons instruction, fighting drills, conditioning, or sparring*.

Training sessions and classes usually consist of four parts.

  1. bowing in
  2. Stretching
  3. warm-up drills & Training
  4. bowing out.

SPARRING

*Sparring practice is not required and is reserved for intermediate and advanced students.
Sparring requires a high level of trust and control - we treat it with the utmost respect and caution.

We practice three types of sparring:

  1. traditional (unpadded no gear) controlled sparring
  2. competition style point sparring
  3. contact sparring
